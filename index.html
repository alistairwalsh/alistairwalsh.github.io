Based in Reservoir, Victoria, Alistair pairs a decade of open science leadership with hands-on expertise in generative AI systems. He helps libraries, universities, and creative studios design responsible AI strategies, orchestrate data-ready pipelines, and prototype agentic tools that deliver measurable impact. From Research Platforms at the University of Melbourne to bespoke client engagements, he meets teams where they are and brings emerging AI capability within reach.

Milestones

Moments that map the journey from neuroscience student to generative AI consultant and open science leader.

  1. 2013

    Launching Aquire for EEG research

    Released a Python client for the Neuroscan Acquire 4.3 server, making it easier to stream multi-channel EEG data into neurofeedback environments.

    Explore Aquire
  2. 2013

    Simulating participants with countZero

    Created a LabStreamingLayer-ready virtual participant to stress-test neurotechnology pipelines before moving to the lab.

    See countZero
  3. 2015

    Scaling Python training across campuses

    Led Software Carpentry workshops at Swinburne University of Technology and the University of Melbourne, expanding the reach of hands-on coding education.

    Swinburne workshop Unimelb workshop
  4. 2020

    Guiding AI literacy for research communities

    Designed and delivered remote-first programs helping academics and librarians explore responsible AI, reproducibility, and data stewardship.

  5. 2019

    Designing modular course starters

    Published a reusable template for interactive Python courses, streamlining delivery for Research Bazaar and partner events.

    Course starter
  6. 2025

    Building library-friendly AI agents

    Most recently, launched agex, experimenting with approachable agentic workflows for collections and cultural institutions.
