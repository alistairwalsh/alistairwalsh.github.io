Neurofeedback researcher · Open science educator

Alistair Walsh

Based in Reservoir, Victoria, Alistair designs neurofeedback and brain-computer interface experiments, builds the tooling that powers them, and teaches researchers how to work confidently with code and data. From Research Platforms at the University of Melbourne to global Software Carpentry cohorts, he helps communities unlock computational thinking while continuing to explore creative technology.

What I'm building

Science, software, and storytelling meet across these core themes.

Neurofeedback & BCI research

Designing experiments that close the loop between brain signals and immersive feedback systems. Hands-on with hardware, signal processing pipelines, and rapid prototyping.

  • Developed Aquire, a Neuroscan Acquire 4.3 client that keeps EEG research flexible.
  • Built countZero, a virtual EEG participant for LabStreamingLayer workflows.

Research platforms & training

Equipping researchers at the University of Melbourne and beyond with the skills to work reproducibly and collaboratively.

  • Rescom ResearchPlatforms@Unimelb facilitator delivering hands-on data intensives.
  • Created workshop materials spanning Python, SQL, data wrangling, and visualization.

Global open science community

Mentoring the next generation of instructors and advocating for inclusive, empowering learning spaces.

  • Software Carpentry Instructor Trainer supporting cohorts across Australasia.
  • Regular contributor to Research Bazaar and allied digital skills programs.

Milestones

Moments that map the journey from neuroscience student to open science leader.

  1. 2013

    Launching Aquire for EEG research

    Released a Python client for the Neuroscan Acquire 4.3 server, making it easier to stream multi-channel EEG data into neurofeedback environments.

    Explore Aquire
  2. 2013

    Simulating participants with countZero

    Created a LabStreamingLayer-ready virtual participant to stress-test neurotechnology pipelines before moving to the lab.

    See countZero
  3. 2015

    Scaling Python training across campuses

    Led Software Carpentry workshops at Swinburne University of Technology and the University of Melbourne, expanding the reach of hands-on coding education.

    Swinburne workshop Unimelb workshop
  4. 2019

    Designing modular course starters

    Published a reusable template for interactive Python courses, streamlining delivery for Research Bazaar and partner events.

    Course starter
  5. 2025

    Building library-friendly AI agents

    Most recently, launched agex, experimenting with approachable agentic workflows for collections and cultural institutions.
